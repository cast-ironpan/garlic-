Garlic

Exploring the different uses of garlic.

Historical Uses of Garlic

1. Mummification: It was used as a perfume to make the bodies smell good. Honey, yogurt, juniper berries, and dates were also used. Garlic and onions eventually were also used. By “taking on the scent of the gods” you would be welcomed into the afterlife. Scent was important in Ancient Egypt it correlated with your wealth and how respected you were.

2. Boosting physical performance: It provided strength and increased work capacity for laborers and helps Greek boost their performance in Olympic Games. One of the first performance “boosting agents”.

3. Medicinal applications: Hippocrates (physician) prescribed garlic for a variety of conditions. In 1564, Lonicerus (famous German doctor/professor) recommended using it to treat many skin diseases.
- Louis Pasteur: wrote about how garlic kills bacteria, said it was good even for some bacteria that had resistances to other things
- 1913: it’s antiseptic properties (preventing spread of infection) kept down cholera
- WW1 (1914-1918): garlic was used to treat German Soldiers as a natural antibiotic. Was used to kill bacteria and clean wounds
- 1918: Garlic kept down typhoid fever and diphtheria
- Lekrek, French physiotherapist, used garlic to prevent the flu (influenza) aka Spanish Fever

Modern Uses of Garlic

Cooking: Good aromatic for cooking different dishes
Medicinal Uses: Dietary supplement for those with heart and blood vessel conditions and problems (e.g. high cholesterol and high blood pressure)
